Prices come in units of 만 (man) = ₩10,000. So 삼만 원 (sam-man won) = ₩30,000.

Tips

Paying in Korea — quick rules

  • Cards work almost everywhere — taxis, convenience stores, most restaurants. Traditional-market stalls (Gukje, Namdaemun, Gwangjang) still prefer cash, so keep some ₩10,000 notes handy.
  • T-money transit cards top up with cash only — at station machines or any convenience store.
  • ATMs: look for machines marked “Global ATM” (inside CU/GS25 stores and banks) — they take foreign cards.
  • No tipping — anywhere, ever. It genuinely confuses people.
  • Instant tax refund: spend ₩15,000+ at participating shops (Olive Young, department stores) and get the tax back at the register — physical passport required.

ClinicMyeongdong

K-derm skin clinic session — glass skin, tourist-style

ShopEverywhere

Olive Young haul — Korea's beauty superstore

Korea's №1 health & beauty chain; the Myeongdong Town flagship is the big one (teens can lose an hour in it). Know before you go: spend ₩15,000+ and get the tax refund instantly at the register — physical passport required (phone photos usually refused). It gets rammed in the evenings, so go in the morning; there are branches all over Seoul, Busan and Jeju too, so nothing is a one-chance buy.

Buy

The 2026 shopping list — what's actually worth buying

The 2026 K-beauty wave is gentle barrier-repair, not harsh actives — which happens to be exactly right for teen skin.

  • Sunscreens (the №1 souvenir): SKIN1004 Hyalu-Cica Water-Fit Sun Serum (sensitive-skin favourite), Dr.G Green Mild Up Sun+ (the dermatologists' gold standard for stressed skin), Torriden watery sun cream, Beauty of Joseon Relief Sun (the classic). All SPF50+ PA++++, none of the white cast of Aussie sunscreen.
  • Cult staples: COSRX snail mucin essence, Torriden DIVE-IN serum, Anua heartleaf toner (very gentle — good teen pick), Laneige lip sleeping mask, sheet masks by the armful (great cheap gifts).
  • For Eva: Korean routine for young skin = gentle cleanser + light moisturiser + sunscreen, done. Patch-test anything new on the inner arm overnight before it goes near a face.
  • Cheap and cheerful: Daiso (₩1,000–5,000 beauty tools, headbands, blotting paper) and Namdaemun's cosmetic stalls for bulk sheet masks.
Pharmacy

Pharmacies (약국) — the practical stuff

  • Spotting one: look for the green cross or 약 (yak) sign — they're everywhere, including several around Myeongdong and Nampo. Typical hours ~09:00–19:00 weekdays, shorter weekends; big-district ones stay open later.
  • How it works: most medicine lives behind the counter — describe the symptom (pharmacists in tourist areas usually manage some English, or use the translate box in the Talk tab). Painkillers, cold & flu, motion-sickness tablets, plasters and stomach remedies are all easy over the counter.
  • After hours: convenience stores (CU/GS25) legally sell a small set of basics 24/7 — acetaminophen (Tylenol), cold tablets, pain-relief patches.
  • Prescriptions: bring enough of any regular medication for the whole trip, in original packaging, plus a copy of the script — Korean pharmacies can't fill foreign prescriptions.
  • Stuck? Call 1330 (24/7 tourist hotline) — they'll find the nearest open pharmacy and interpret for you. Note: Olive Young is cosmetics only, not a pharmacy.
